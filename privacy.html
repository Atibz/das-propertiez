For more detailed information on how we manage data security and compliance, you may refer to our full Data Protection Policy.

What is Personal Data?

Under the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), Personal Data refers to any information that relates to an identified or identifiable individual (referred to as a "data subject"). An individual is considered identifiable if they can be recognized directly or indirectly through identifiers such as:

  • Name
  • Identification number
  • Location data
  • Online identifiers
  • Physical, physiological, genetic, mental, economic, cultural or social characteristics

At DAS Properties, we collect and process personal data only as necessary to deliver our services, communicate effectively and maintain compliance with relevant laws.

Special Categories of Personal Data

The GDPR also defines certain types of personal data as “special categories,” which require a higher level of protection due to their sensitive nature. These include information related to:

  • Racial or ethnic origin
  • Political opinions
  • Religious or philosophical beliefs
  • Trade union membership
  • Genetic data
  • Biometric data
  • Health data
  • Sex life or sexual orientation

DAS Properties may process both general and special categories of personal data when necessary to fulfill specific services or regulatory obligations. Where sensitive data is required, we will always request your explicit consent and clearly explain the reason for collecting such information and how it will be used.

How Long We Store Your Personal Data

DAS Properties stores personal data in accordance with the principles of the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). We are committed to maintaining accurate and up-to-date information and will not retain your data longer than necessary for the purposes for which it was collected.

Where legal or regulatory requirements exist, we comply with specified retention periods. In other instances, we use professional discretion and internal policy to determine appropriate retention timelines in line with our operational needs.

Below are the general retention periods for data we collect in relation to our property services (sales and lettings):

Retention Periods

  • Legitimate interest – up to 7 years

    For example, where a customer or client has registered interest in buying, selling, letting or renting a property.

    Basis: Ensures we maintain accurate service history and client communications.

  • Property Ombudsman Code of Practice – 6 years

    We are required to maintain full written records of property transactions, including offers and communications, for a minimum of six years.

  • Money Laundering Regulations 2017 – 5 years

    Records related to identity verification and financial transactions must be retained for five years to ensure regulatory compliance.
